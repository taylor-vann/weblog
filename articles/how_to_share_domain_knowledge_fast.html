How to share domain knowledge quickly

Here is a paper based solution we used at Meta to share domain knowledge across our team.

It's pretty boring.

Unless building your coworkers into a cache sounds interesting.

Or maybe you enjoy watching some index cards and a couple rules compound into a more supportive work environment.

This exercise works best for small teams.

Required materials:

  • index cards
  • coworkers
  • permanent markers
  • a parental figure (tech lead or manager)

What are we talking about?

Domain knowledge is the context required to explain the purpose of software architecture; how it operates and performs.

It is critical to successful software.

Domain knowledge separates generalists from specialists. It's a culture of delivery and availability. It's a vector with magnitude and direction that looks an awful lot like momentum and vision.

Developers use domain knowledge to create meaningful contributions. And their cohort can easily understand those contributions. It's the why not the how.

Without domain knowledge, software becomes a patchwork held together by duct tape and dollar bills.

And that's expensive.
